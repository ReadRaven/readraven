Our Values

Reading habits are intimate. We will never sell or share information about you to anyone. Ever.

Web startups are fickle. We are building for us, for you, and forever. We will never sell to anyone else. Period.

Your time is precious, and curating feeds is expensive. We strongly believe in open data and data portability and will never lock you in.

We plan to charge $5 per month for as long as we can. We think it's a fair, sustainable price that will allow us to build a clean, crisp product for you, forever.

* * *

Why are you doing this?

The shuttering of Google Reader was as painful for us as it was for everyone else. When we took a look around at the alternatives, we didn't really find exactly what we wanted. So we sketched out a plan to build something based on our values, which include user privacy, longevity, and fairness.

We are dismayed by the increasing number of sites who make money by selling data about their users for the sake of advertising. In today's world your data is you, and the thought of being packaged, sliced, diced, and sold to 3rd parties is nauseating.

As well, it's difficult to figure out which sites to trust with investment of your most precious resources: time and attention. Sites that grow fast and cash out squander this resource for everyone and it's heartbreaking.

We're building to avoid that problem and invite you to join us.


Who are you?

Alex Chiang and Paul Hummer met at Canonical, the sponsors of Ubuntu. We both believe strongly in building software for human beings, and the demise of Google Reader was a great opportunity to create something for users, not advertisers.


Why are you charging money?

There are many free, great RSS readers in the market today if you're not interested in paying for Readraven.

Our promise is that if you sign up for Readraven, we will keep this site alive for as long as humanly possible to protect your investment of time, which is far more valuable than money. We never sell to anyone else and we'll provide a safe haven on an internet that is ever attempting to track your personal habits to monetize them.


What happens if I stop paying?

We believe in data portability and we will provide you a full export of all your data. We'll also keep your data around for a little while to make it convenient in case you decide to come back, or an expunge option where we'll make sure we wipe everything about you from our servers, forever.

If you are ever unhappy with Readraven, please email help@readraven.com and we will provide an immediate, no questions asked refund.


What's your roadmap?

Our biggest goal between now and July 1 is to build a perfect importer for Google Reader data. We think it's already pretty good, but if you find any issues, please email us at help@readraven.com.

After July 1, we plan on re-implementing the best parts of Reader such as sharing, commenting, and the like. We also have quite a number of ideas to add features that Reader never had. Building a paid site gives us freedom to build things that sites with other business models would never allow.
